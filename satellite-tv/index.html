Problem

Finding underserved markets before competitors did

UPC Broadband needed to expand satellite TV coverage but had limited budget to target all settlements equally. The challenge was identifying which areas had the highest potential — high household income, low competitor presence, and proximity to existing customers — without expensive field research.

This was an early-stage data-driven project in 2007, when the tooling was limited and the approach had to be built from scratch.

Approach

Integrating three data sources

  • Competitor cable coverage maps — scraped and geocoded by settlement
  • Household income data — sourced from public statistical records
  • Existing UPC customer locations — from internal CRM database
SQL Server · settlement_scoring.sql
-- Score each settlement by expansion potential
SELECT
  s.settlement_id,
  s.settlement_name,
  s.county,
  s.population,
  i.avg_household_income,
  c.competitor_coverage_pct,
  COUNT(cu.customer_id)       AS existing_customers,

  -- Composite score: high income, low competition, existing base
  (i.avg_household_income / 1000)
    * (1 - c.competitor_coverage_pct)
    * LOG(COUNT(cu.customer_id) + 1)
                              AS expansion_score

FROM settlements s
LEFT JOIN income_data i
  ON s.settlement_id = i.settlement_id
LEFT JOIN competitor_coverage c
  ON s.settlement_id = c.settlement_id
LEFT JOIN customers cu
  ON s.settlement_id = cu.settlement_id
GROUP BY
  s.settlement_id, s.settlement_name, s.county,
  s.population, i.avg_household_income,
  c.competitor_coverage_pct
ORDER BY expansion_score DESC

Visualisation in MapInfo

The scored settlements were exported and visualised as a heatmap in MapInfo, layered over competitor coverage boundaries. This allowed the sales team to visually identify clusters of high-potential, underserved markets and plan targeted field campaigns.
