Segment definitions

How segments are defined

Segments are based on tenure — the number of years between a customer's first and last subscription date. This approach focuses analysis on the most commercially important question: at what point in the customer lifecycle does churn happen?

New Customer
Tenure = 0 years. First subscription, never renewed. The highest-risk group — over half will not return for a second year.
Second Year Customer
Tenure = 1–2 years. Has renewed at least once. The crucial turning point — churn drops sharply from new customer levels.
High Value Customer
Tenure = 3+ years. Long-term member. Lower churn but slightly higher than second year — may reflect a loyalty plateau.

Query

Churn rate by customer segment

BigQuery SQL · churn_by_segment.sql
SELECT
  customer_segment,
  COUNT(*)                                 AS total_customers,
  COUNTIF(is_churned = 1)                 AS churned,
  COUNTIF(is_churned = 0)                 AS retained,
  ROUND(COUNTIF(is_churned = 1) * 100.0
    / COUNT(*), 1)                          AS churn_rate_pct
FROM `lewagon-457508`.home_exchange_reporting.v_customer_activity
GROUP BY 1
ORDER BY
  CASE customer_segment
    WHEN 'New Customer'         THEN 1
    WHEN 'Second Year Customer' THEN 2
    WHEN 'High Value Customer'  THEN 3
  END
Key findings
Year one is the biggest risk
56.6% of new customers do not renew — more than half are lost before experiencing a second year of membership
The year 1 → 2 inflection
Churn drops 18 percentage points from New to Second Year — the sharpest change in the entire customer lifecycle
High value plateau
High Value customers churn slightly more than Second Year (40.8% vs 38.5%) — suggesting loyalty plateaus after year 2 without active engagement
Largest segment is High Value
28,617 High Value customers — the biggest segment — shows the platform does retain a strong long-term base once members pass year two
Activity as a churn predictor

The more you exchange, the more you stay

Exchange activity is the single strongest predictor of renewal. The relationship is perfectly linear — every step up in activity reduces churn by approximately 10 percentage points.

Churn by geography

Europe and North America churn almost identically

The widely held assumption that American subscribers behave differently from European ones is not supported by the churn data. Both continents churn at ~43% — the difference is negligible. Higher churn in Asia and South America likely reflects lower brand awareness and less mature exchange communities.

Recommendations

Where to focus retention efforts

  • Onboarding campaign: Push new subscribers toward their first completed exchange within 90 days — activity is the strongest predictor of renewal
  • Year one re-engagement: Identify subscribers approaching their renewal date with zero completed exchanges and trigger targeted campaigns
  • High value nurturing: The slight churn uptick in long-term members suggests a loyalty programme or milestone recognition could prevent attrition at the plateau
