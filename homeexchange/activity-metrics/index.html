Activity metrics by segment

Medians used throughout to avoid distortion from extreme outliers. Success rate is the percentage of exchange requests that result in a completed exchange.

Segment Customers Median requests Avg completed* Median nights Success rate Never completed
New Customer 19,538 42 3.87 9 6.3% 26.6%
Second Year Customer 22,566 69 5.84 16 4.9% 24.4%
High Value Customer 28,609 54 5.06 13 4.2% 31.6%

* avg completed = total completed exchanges / customers with at least one completion

Query
BigQuery SQL · activity_metrics.sql
SELECT
  customer_segment,
  COUNT(*)                                           AS total_customers,
  APPROX_QUANTILES(total_requests, 100)[OFFSET(50)]   AS median_requests,
  ROUND(SUM(completed_exchanges) * 1.0
    / NULLIF(COUNTIF(completed_exchanges > 0), 0),
    2)                                                AS avg_per_active_user,
  APPROX_QUANTILES(total_nights, 100)[OFFSET(50)]     AS median_nights,
  ROUND(AVG(success_rate_pct), 1)                   AS avg_success_rate,
  ROUND(COUNTIF(completed_exchanges = 0) * 100.0
    / COUNT(*), 1)                                  AS pct_never_completed
FROM `lewagon-457508`.home_exchange_reporting.v_customer_activity
GROUP BY 1
ORDER BY
  CASE customer_segment
    WHEN 'New Customer'         THEN 1
    WHEN 'Second Year Customer' THEN 2
    WHEN 'High Value Customer'  THEN 3
  END
Key findings
Second Year most active
5.84 avg completed exchanges — more than High Value (5.06). Peak engagement happens in years 1–2, not later
High Value stays longest
Median 13 nights vs 9 for New customers. Experienced members plan longer, more deliberate trips
Platform matching challenge
Only ~5% of requests result in a completed exchange across all segments — the platform has a significant supply-demand matching gap
31.6% of High Value never completed
Surprisingly, nearly a third of long-term members never completed an exchange — likely explains the churn plateau seen in year 3+
Home listings & engagement

Multi-home users are platform anchors

Users who list more than one home are significantly more active on both sides of the platform — they travel more as guests and host more in return. The symmetry between guest and host activity is remarkable: 3+ home users average 7.7 completed exchanges as guests and 7.7 as hosts.
