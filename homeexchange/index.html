Key findings
Lockdown impact — immediate
Domestic share jumped from 41% in Feb 2020 to 72% by April 2020 — within just 6 weeks of the first restrictions
Intercontinental — near zero
Intercontinental exchanges collapsed to just 3.2% in June 2020 — from a pre-Covid baseline of ~24%
Second wave — Feb 2021
Domestic peaked again at 82.1% in February 2021 as European countries re-entered lockdown
Permanent behavioural shift
By end of 2022, domestic exchanges stabilised at ~48-50% — higher than the pre-Covid 40% baseline, suggesting lasting behaviour change
