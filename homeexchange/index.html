HomeExchange
Customer Behaviour & Retention Analysis

HomeExchange is a French home-swap travel platform where members pay €149/year to access peer-to-peer house exchanges. This analysis covers 70,726 subscribers and 11.9 million exchange records from 2019–2022 — exploring who stays, who leaves, where they go, and what the business should do about it.

Segment definitions

How segments are defined

Segments are based on tenure — the number of years between a customer's first and last subscription date. This approach focuses analysis on the most commercially important question: at what point in the customer lifecycle does churn happen?

New Customer
Tenure = 0 years. First subscription, never renewed. The highest-risk group — over half will not return for a second year.
Second Year Customer
Tenure = 1–2 years. Has renewed at least once. The crucial turning point — churn drops sharply from new customer levels.
High Value Customer
Tenure = 3+ years. Long-term member. Lower churn but slightly higher than second year — may reflect a loyalty plateau.

Query

Churn rate by customer segment

BigQuery SQL · churn_by_segment.sql ▾ show query 
SELECT
  customer_segment,
  COUNT(*)                                 AS total_customers,
  COUNTIF(is_churned = 1)                 AS churned,
  COUNTIF(is_churned = 0)                 AS retained,
  ROUND(COUNTIF(is_churned = 1) * 100.0
    / COUNT(*), 1)                          AS churn_rate_pct
FROM `lewagon-457508`.home_exchange_reporting.v_customer_activity
GROUP BY 1
ORDER BY
  CASE customer_segment
    WHEN 'New Customer'         THEN 1
    WHEN 'Second Year Customer' THEN 2
    WHEN 'High Value Customer'  THEN 3
  END

Churn by geography

Europe and North America churn almost identically

The widely held assumption that American subscribers behave differently from European ones is not supported by the churn data. Both continents churn at ~43% — the difference is negligible. Higher churn in Asia and South America likely reflects lower brand awareness and less mature exchange communities.

Recommendations

Where to focus retention efforts

  • Onboarding campaign: Push new subscribers toward their first completed exchange within 90 days — activity is the strongest predictor of renewal
  • Year one re-engagement: Identify subscribers approaching their renewal date with zero completed exchanges and trigger targeted campaigns
  • High value nurturing: The slight churn uptick in long-term members suggests a loyalty programme or milestone recognition could prevent attrition at the plateau

Query

SELECT
  customer_segment,
  COUNT(*)                                           AS total_customers,
  APPROX_QUANTILES(total_requests, 100)[OFFSET(50)]   AS median_requests,
  ROUND(SUM(completed_exchanges) * 1.0
    / NULLIF(COUNTIF(completed_exchanges > 0), 0),
    2)                                                AS avg_per_active_user,
  APPROX_QUANTILES(total_nights, 100)[OFFSET(50)]     AS median_nights,
  ROUND(AVG(success_rate_pct), 1)                   AS avg_success_rate,
  ROUND(COUNTIF(completed_exchanges = 0) * 100.0
    / COUNT(*), 1)                                  AS pct_never_completed
FROM `lewagon-457508`.home_exchange_reporting.v_customer_activity
GROUP BY 1
ORDER BY
  CASE customer_segment
    WHEN 'New Customer'         THEN 1
    WHEN 'Second Year Customer' THEN 2
    WHEN 'High Value Customer'  THEN 3
  END

Business context — subscriber growth

Acquisition collapsed — retention held the business together

The platform grew steadily from 2005 to 2019, peaking at 17,153 new subscribers in 2019. When Covid hit, new acquisition dropped 42% to 9,898 in 2020 and fell further to 7,414 in 2021. Yet total subscriber numbers kept rising — because existing members stayed. During Covid, retention was the only growth engine.

06 — Recommendations
From data to action
Six concrete recommendations tied directly to the findings — ordered by estimated retention impact.
Recommendations
01 — Highest priority
Drive new subscribers to their first completed exchange within 90 days
New customers churn at 56.6% — the highest of any segment. The data shows that members who never complete an exchange churn at 67%, while those who complete even one drop to 47%. Getting a subscriber to their first successful exchange is the single most impactful retention action available.

Trigger a structured onboarding sequence: personalised home match suggestions at day 7, a first-exchange guide at day 14, and a direct re-engagement message at day 45 for members who have made no exchange requests.
Evidence Never requested: 67% churn · Requested never completed: 56.1% · High activity: 26.5% churn — a 40-point gap driven purely by exchange activity
Target Reduce new customer churn from 56.6% → below 45% by increasing first-exchange completion rate within the subscription year
Product Marketing Retention
02 — Highest impact per euro spent
Scale the promotional discount programme — it is the strongest retention lever in the dataset
Subscribers who received a promotional discount churn at just 7.0% — compared to 46.8% for those who received no incentive. This is a 39.8-point reduction from a single intervention. The combined effect of referral and promotion produces 4.9% churn — effectively near-zero attrition.

Currently only 1,889 subscribers received a promotion with no referral, and 349 received both. These numbers are far too small relative to the 70,000+ subscriber base. A targeted promotion strategy — especially at renewal time — would have outsized impact.
Evidence No referral, no promo: 46.8% churn · Promotion only: 7.0% · Referral + promo: 4.9% — the largest single churn reduction in the entire analysis
Target Offer a targeted renewal discount to at-risk subscribers (low activity, approaching renewal with zero recent exchanges) and measure 12-month retention lift
Marketing Retention
03
Invest in growing the referral programme — referred members churn 12 points less
Referred subscribers churn at 34.2% vs 46.8% for organic subscribers — a 12.6-point improvement without any promotional cost. Yet only 6,388 out of 70,726 subscribers (9%) were referred. The referral channel is dramatically underutilised.

Introduce a structured member referral programme with incentives for both the referrer and the new member. Members who have completed 3+ exchanges are the ideal referrers — they are the most engaged and have the most to say about the platform's value.
Evidence Referral churn: 34.2% vs baseline 46.8% · Only 9% of subscribers were referred · Referral + promo achieves lowest churn (4.9%) in entire dataset
Target Grow referred subscriber share from 9% → 20% within 2 years. Each percentage point shift represents ~700 additional low-churn subscribers
Growth Marketing Retention
04
Trigger a second-home listing prompt after the first completed exchange
Users who list multiple homes are dramatically more engaged on both sides of the platform. 3+ home users complete 7.7 exchanges as guests and 7.7 as hosts — perfect symmetry, and 2.4x more than single-home users. They also churn 6 points less.

Critically, 60% of 3+ home hosts are paying subscribers, vs only 14.5% of single-home hosts — listing a second home is the strongest conversion signal in the data. After a member's first successful exchange, prompt them to add their second property if they have one. This single action could dramatically improve both engagement and subscription conversion among casual hosts.
Evidence 1 home: 44.6% churn, 3.2 avg exchanges · 3+ homes: 38.6% churn, 7.7 avg exchanges · 60% of multi-home hosts are subscribers vs 14.5% single-home
Target Increase multi-home listing rate among subscribers. Test a post-exchange prompt and measure second-home listing rate and 6-month retention vs control group
Product Retention Growth
05
Prioritise geographic expansion only where local exchange density is sufficient
Asia and South America churn at 52–53% — roughly 10 points above Europe and North America. The most likely cause is insufficient local community density: members in these markets cannot find enough nearby exchange partners, reducing platform value before they've had a chance to experience it.

Geographic expansion should follow a community-first model: build local listing density in a target city or region before marketing subscriptions. A minimum viable density threshold — for example 500 active listings within a metropolitan area — should be reached before subscription campaigns are activated.
Evidence Asia: 53.2% churn (680 subscribers) · South America: 52.4% (1,005) · Europe: 43.8% (46,428) · North America: 43.3% (17,074)
Target Define a minimum listing density threshold for new market activation. Measure churn in pilot cities at launch vs 12 months after density threshold is reached
Growth Product
06
Use Covid recovery data to plan seasonal and intercontinental campaigns
Domestic exchanges peaked at 83.5% during Covid restrictions and have since stabilised at ~48% — permanently above the pre-Covid 40% baseline. Intercontinental travel, which generates the longest stays (avg 7.8 nights vs 5.4 domestic), has partially recovered but remains below 2019 levels.

Two opportunities: first, develop domestic exchange campaigns targeted at members who have only ever exchanged locally — they are the most likely to try continental or intercontinental exchanges as confidence returns. Second, intercontinental exchanges are the highest-value stays — a targeted campaign highlighting long-stay international options could reactivate members who have been dormant since 2020.
Evidence Pre-Covid domestic: 40% · Covid peak: 83.5% · 2022 new normal: ~48% · Intercontinental avg nights: 7.8 vs domestic 5.4 — 44% longer stays
Target Grow intercontinental exchange share back toward pre-Covid 22-24% by 2024. Identify domestic-only members with 3+ exchanges as the prime intercontinental upsell audience
